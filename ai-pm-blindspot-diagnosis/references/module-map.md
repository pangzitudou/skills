# AI PM Blindspot Module Map

Use this fixed map for version 1. Ask every module. Do not skip.

| # | Module | Diagnostic Focus |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Business problem definition | Whether the PM can narrow the user, pain, learning job, and exclusion boundaries. |
| 2 | AI necessity | Whether the PM can justify where AI is needed and where simpler mechanisms beat AI. |
| 3 | User workflow | Whether the PM understands the full student learning loop and parent supervision loop. |
| 4 | Model capability boundaries | Whether the PM knows what the model cannot be trusted to do unaided. |
| 5 | Prompt and instruction design | Whether the PM can shape tutoring behavior without relying on vague role prompts. |
| 6 | Personalized learning path | Whether the PM can define mastery, diagnosis, progression, and review logic. |
| 7 | Agent and tool use | Whether the PM knows which parts need tools, retrieval, solvers, or structured systems. |
| 8 | Data quality and data permissions | Whether the PM can separate needed data from unsafe or unjustified collection. |
| 9 | Learning outcome evaluation and evals | Whether the PM measures student learning, not just model answer quality. |
| 10 | Logs and feedback loops | Whether the PM can turn real usage into issue discovery and product improvement. |
| 11 | Hallucination and wrong explanations | Whether the PM has a plan for incorrect reasoning that harms learning. |
| 12 | Safety and prompt injection | Whether the PM sees adversarial student input and unsafe behavior as product risks. |
| 13 | Privacy and minor compliance | Whether the PM handles children, parent rights, consent, retention, and data use. |
| 14 | Cost, latency, and reliability | Whether the PM can reason about unit economics and operational constraints. |
| 15 | Rollout, monitoring, and rollback | Whether the PM can launch safely and reverse bad outcomes quickly. |
| 16 | Business metrics and ROI | Whether the PM connects learning value, parent willingness to pay, and unit economics. |

## Relevance Rule

For this fixed K12 math AI tutor case, every module is relevant.

If the user claims a module is not applicable:

1. Ask for one-sentence reason.
2. Accept only if the reason shows a clear product scope boundary.
3. If the reason is weak, evasive, or based on misunderstanding, mark it as a blind spot.
